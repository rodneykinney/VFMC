VFMC is a virtual FMC attempt tool. Use it to refine your FMC strategy and practice case recognition.

Enter scramble("...") to load a scramble.
Enter scramble to generate a new random scramble.

Solving Individual Steps

Move from step to step by entering the name of the step you want to work on.
Available steps are: eofb,eorl,eoud,drud,drrl,drfb,htr,fr,finish,insertions

Display

When starting a step, the cube will reset its orientation
putting the DR axis on top and the EO axis in front.
Change the orientation by entering x, y', z2, etc.
Fine-tune the orientation by dragging the mouse in the display.

By default, only the bad edges and corners are drawn in the display.
Use the "Display Preferences" to change what is drawn for each step.

Press the Ctrl/Option key to temporarily hide the front faces of the cube.

Moves

Enter moves (e.g. u' r2 f) to apply them to the cube.
Alternatively, double-click on the final set of moves to the right of the display to edit the moves directly.

Enter reset to return to the beginning of the current step.
Enter niss to switch between normal and inverse scrambles.

Solver

Enter solve at any point to find the shortest unknown solution to the current step.
Enter solve(n) to find the next n solutions (up to 50 at at time)

Enter mallard("...", n) to solve a chain of steps simultaneously using a subset of the mallard syntax.
Available steps: EO, DR, HTR, FR, FIN
Available options: max, abs-max, niss
Examples: "EO[max=5] > DR[abs-max=11]", or "HTR > FIN"

Building a Full Solution

Enter save to save the current cube to the list of solutions at the bottom.
Enter comment("...") to specify a comment for the current step
Enter done to mark the current step with a strike-through, or forget to remove it entirely

The steps that are active in the display are drawn in bold.
Click on any step at the bottom to see the steps that precede or follow it.
Double click to load a step and start working on it.
Hit delete to forget the selected step entirely

The insertions step rewrites the solution to be all on normal and brings up a dialog showing the remaining bad pieces. The view changes depending on the cursor position, as if you had rniss-ed at that position. You can widen moves by adding 'w' and otherwise edit the moves. The edits will be automatically converted into insertion markers when saving.

Saving/Loading Your Session

Enter save_session("...") to save all of your activity so far to a log file.
Enter load_session("...") to replay a saved session.
